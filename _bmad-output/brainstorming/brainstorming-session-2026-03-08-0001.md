## Technique Execution Results

### Phase 1: Dream Fusion Laboratory

**Interactive Focus:** Unlocking the emotional north star before any constraints. Started with feeling/atmosphere, then layered in real assets: the color palette, the venue, the church, the monogram, the love story.

**Key Breakthroughs:**
- The venue itself IS the design metaphor: *walking down the aisle* as the scroll experience
- The "Ten Years. One More Day." concept crystallized from the Jan 7 anniversary / Jan 8 wedding date discovery
- The color palette names (Ninangs, Mothers, Flower Girls) revealed this as a deeply Filipino wedding — adding cultural richness
- The Mt. Fuji proposal creates the story arc: Philippines (ordinary) → Fuji (extraordinary) → Batangas (home forever)

**Energy Level:** High throughout — every new asset (venue photo, palette, church, monogram) unlocked a cascade of ideas.

### Phase 2: Cross-Pollination

**Domains raided:** Luxury fashion (Jacquemus, Bottega Veneta), A24 Films, Aman Resorts / The Opposite House, Coachella / music festivals, Japanese Zen design

**Key Breakthroughs:**
- **Aman Resorts** resonated most strongly — pure Filipino *pagmamalasakit* translated to digital hospitality
- Film industry gave us the "end credits" entourage concept — every ninong/ninang as a headliner
- Japanese design gave us permission to use radical negative space and the wabi-sabi imperfection
- Festival thinking reframed the entourage as a "lineup" and introduced the "pre-wedding hype drops" content strategy

### Phase 3: SCAMPER Method

**Applied to:** RSVP form, venue map, photo gallery, countdown, hero, entourage list, navigation, gift registry, wedding date display, confirmation messages

**Key Breakthroughs:**
- Eliminating the navigation menu entirely — forcing the narrative scroll
- Reversing the timeline direction (end → beginning, the classroom as final reveal)
- *"We've been waiting for you"* as the RSVP confirmation (Aman DNA applied to the most transactional moment)
- The monogram as loader — turning the most annoying UX moment into a branded ceremony

## Top 3 Priorities & Action Plans

### Priority 1: Admin Mode *(new insight — emerged in organization phase)*
**Why it matters:** The site needs to be managed for 1–2 years without a developer. Personalized guest links, RSVP dashboard, content drops, and announcement publishing all require admin infrastructure.

**Next Steps:**
1. Choose tech stack: headless CMS (Sanity/Contentful) or custom `/admin` route
2. Define editable content: RSVP data, announcements, photos, section toggles, guest list
3. Build RSVP dashboard first (highest-frequency task)
4. Design the admin UI in the wedding aesthetic — even internal tools should feel considered

**Must-scope admin features:** RSVP export for caterer · personalized link generator · announcement publisher · photo/content uploader

### Priority 2: Smooth Mobile Experience
**Why it matters:** 90%+ of guests will open this on their phone in the Philippines. This IS the product.

**Next Steps:**
1. Design mobile-first — every decision starts at 390px width
2. Prioritize: conversational RSVP, swipe interactions, haptic confirmation, scroll-only navigation
3. Performance budget: under 3 seconds on Philippine LTE networks
4. Test on mid-range Android (majority of Filipino guests)

**Critical decisions:** iOS audio autoplay workaround · scroll-snap cross-browser behavior · image compression for venue photography

### Priority 3: Elegant Palette Use
**Why it matters:** 8 named colors with role assignments is a design system, not just a palette. It needs rules to stay cohesive across 1–2 years of updates.

**Next Steps:**
1. Map each color to a specific semantic function (background, CTA, text, accent, divider)
2. Build palette as CSS custom properties from day one — never hardcode hex values
3. Implement palette-as-wayfinding (#65) — each section's accent = its color role
4. Day/night mode (#11) uses the same system, weighted differently by time of day
